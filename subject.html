What exactly is D&D?

Dungeons and Dragons or commonly known as D&D is a pen and paper roleplaying game played with phyical or digital dice. It is often played with a dungeon master(who creates a story and world) and a party consisted of a few players (who control characters that live in the world). The story is often set in a fantasy setting and ends when the party's characters die or defeat the BBEG(Big Bad Evil Guy/Girl). Short video explaining D&D.

Basics of Character Creation

When creating a character, a player chooses from a list of official classes, backgrounds, and races or some that a dungeon master creates or homebrews. After choosing a class, a player then rolls 6d20 dice to determine attributes of a character. Next a player chooses languages and abilities they are profiecient in. After that, a character can choose from a default set of items or buy them. Then, a player chooses spells and sets up their HP and combat stats. Finally, the player sets up the personality of their character. Short video explaining character creation.

How does combat work?

During combat, each character rolls a dice for intiative to determine which character goes first. During each turn, players can choose to move and perform a various amount of actions, including attacking. Often times when actions are used, the character will roll a die to determine whether or not the action succeeds. After a character completes their action, it is now the next initiative's turn. This continues until all enemies or players die, are knocked unconcious, or flee. Short video explaining combat.
